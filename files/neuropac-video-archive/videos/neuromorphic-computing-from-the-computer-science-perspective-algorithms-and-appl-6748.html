Neuromorphic Computing from the Computer Science Perspective – Algorithms and Applications

Schuman Catherine•2025

NHR PerfLab seminar talk on April 1, 2025 Speaker: Catherine Schuman, Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, University of Tennessee Title: Neuromorphic Computing from the Computer Science Perspective – Algorithms and Applications Slides: https://hpc.fau.de/files/2025/04/NHR-PerfLab_April2025.pdf Abstract: Neuromorphic computing is a popular technology for the future of computing. Much of the focus in neuromorphic computing research and development has focused on new architectures, devices, and materials, rather than in the software, algorithms, and applications of these systems. In this talk, I will overview the field of neuromorphic from the computer science perspective. I will give an introduction to spiking neural networks, as well as some of the most common algorithms used in the field. Finally, I will discuss the potential for using neuromorphic systems in real-world applications, from scientific data analysis to autonomous vehicles.
